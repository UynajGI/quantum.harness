# arXiv:2302.04919 Variational Benchmarks

This is non-actionable reference material for problem skills. Use it for facts, definitions, and citations; do not use it as a workflow, task catalog, or reading path.

## Scope

The paper benchmarks variational ground-state approximations for strongly correlated lattice and impurity models. It introduces the V-score as an empirical, dimensionless accuracy metric combining variational energy and energy variance.

## Benchmark Models

- Transverse-field Ising model:
  `H = J sum_<ij> sigma_i^z sigma_j^z + Gamma sum_i sigma_i^x`.
- Heisenberg model:
  `H = J sum_<ij>,a sigma_i^a sigma_j^a`.
- `J1-J2` model:
  nearest-neighbor plus next-nearest-neighbor Heisenberg couplings.
- Spinless `t-V` model:
  hopping plus nearest-neighbor density repulsion at fixed fermion number.
- Hubbard model:
  spinful hopping plus onsite interaction at fixed `N_up = N_down`.
- Generalized Hubbard variants:
  second-neighbor hopping and nearest/next-nearest density repulsions.
- Anderson impurity models:
  local interacting impurity coupled to a non-interacting bath.
- Multiorbital Kanamori impurity models:
  density-density, spin-flip, and pair-hopping terms.

## V-score

```text
V-score = N Var(E) / (E - E_inf)^2
Var(E) = <H^2> - <H>^2
E = <psi|H|psi>
E_inf = Tr(H) / dim(H)
```

`N` is the number of degrees of freedom: usually number of spins for spin models and particle number for fixed-particle fermion models. `E_inf` is the infinite-temperature/random-state reference energy in the chosen Hilbert subspace.

## Difficulty Signals

The paper reports low V-scores for many 1D and unfrustrated spin problems, especially where DMRG or sign-problem-free QMC is effective. Harder regimes include frustrated geometries such as triangular, kagome, and pyrochlore Heisenberg models, square-lattice `J1-J2`, two-dimensional Hubbard regimes, and multiband impurity models.
