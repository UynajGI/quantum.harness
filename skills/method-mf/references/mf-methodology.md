# Mean-Field Methods — Reproduction-Grade Methodology Reference

Scope: Hartree–Fock / unrestricted Hartree–Fock (UHF) for lattice fermions
(Hubbard, t–J) and Weiss / decoupling mean-field for spin models
(transverse-field Ising, Heisenberg). Self-consistent order parameters and
phase diagrams. Companion to `skills/method-mf/SKILL.md` — the skill owns routing
and pitfalls; this file owns the *algorithm* in reproducible detail.

All math is UTF-8 unicode. Sign and factor conventions are stated explicitly
because mean-field results are convention-sensitive (factors of 2 from spin-½ vs
Pauli operators, and from bond-counting z vs z/2).

---

## 1. Overview — what mean-field does

Mean-field theory replaces a many-body interaction by an effective single-particle
field that each particle feels from the *average* of all the others, with that
average fixed **self-consistently**. A quartic (interacting) Hamiltonian becomes a
quadratic (free) one parameterized by order parameters; the order parameters are
then recomputed from the free-Hamiltonian ground state and iterated to a fixed
point. Concretely:

- **Fermions (Hartree–Fock):** decouple `U n↑ n↓` into terms linear in the
  densities ⟨n↑⟩, ⟨n↓⟩ (and off-diagonal ⟨c†↑c↓⟩ for spiral/SDW order). The
  resulting quadratic Hamiltonian is diagonalized (in k-space if translationally
  invariant, in real space for UHF on finite/disordered lattices).
- **Spins (Weiss):** decouple Sᵢ·Sⱼ into a single spin coupled to the *molecular
  field* set by its neighbors' average moments ⟨S⟩.

**Good for.** Qualitative phase diagrams; identifying candidate broken symmetries
(ferromagnet, antiferromagnet/SDW, charge order, BCS pairing); cheap order-parameter
and T_c estimates; *reference states* to seed correlated methods (DMRG, VMC, QMC).
It is the right **first pass**: O(minutes) on a laptop, and it tells you which
ordered states are even competitive.

**Bad for.** Quantitative accuracy. Mean-field **overestimates order** —
larger order parameters, higher critical temperatures (often by tens of percent;
e.g. ~76% high T_c for the 2D square-lattice Ising ferromagnet) — because it
discards fluctuations. It **misses quantum/thermal fluctuations** entirely, so in
1D it predicts **false long-range order and false finite-T transitions** that the
Mermin–Wagner theorem and exact solutions forbid. Treat every mean-field T_c and
order parameter as an **upper bound on ordering**, never as the answer.

## 2. Hartree–Fock decoupling

### 2.1 General decoupling of a quartic term

For a two-body interaction the mean-field (Hartree–Fock) approximation replaces a
quartic operator by its **Wick contractions about the trial state**, keeping terms
linear in fluctuations and dropping the quadratic-fluctuation remainder. For
operators A,B,C,D (here products of c†, c):

  AB ≈ ⟨A⟩B + A⟨B⟩ − ⟨A⟩⟨B⟩   (the operator identity AB = ⟨A⟩B + A⟨B⟩ − ⟨A⟩⟨B⟩ + δA δB, dropping δA δB)

Applied to a density–density interaction `c†ₐ cₐ c†_b c_b` there are two channels:

- **Hartree (direct):** contract within each density,
  ⟨c†ₐ cₐ⟩ c†_b c_b + c†ₐ cₐ ⟨c†_b c_b⟩ − ⟨c†ₐ cₐ⟩⟨c†_b c_b⟩.
- **Fock (exchange):** contract across,
  −⟨c†ₐ c_b⟩ c†_b cₐ − c†ₐ c_b ⟨c†_b cₐ⟩ + ⟨c†ₐ c_b⟩⟨c†_b cₐ⟩.

For the *on-site* Hubbard interaction the two electrons sharing a site have
**opposite spin** (Pauli), so the same-site Fock channel vanishes and only the
Hartree (density) channel survives at the single site. A nontrivial Fock channel
reappears as soon as off-diagonal-in-spin order ⟨c†↑c↓⟩ is allowed (spin spiral)
or for non-local interactions.

### 2.2 Hubbard U n↑ n↓

Hubbard Hamiltonian (sign convention: hopping `−t`, repulsion `U>0`):

  H = −t Σ_{⟨ij⟩,σ} (c†_{iσ} c_{jσ} + h.c.) + U Σ_i n_{i↑} n_{i↓} − μ Σ_{i,σ} n_{iσ}

Decouple the on-site term in the density (collinear) channel. Define site order
parameters
  nᵢ = ⟨n_{i↑}⟩ + ⟨n_{i↓}⟩   (local charge density),
  mᵢ = ⟨n_{i↑}⟩ − ⟨n_{i↓}⟩   (local magnetization, z-component).

Then

  U n_{i↑} n_{i↓} ≈ U( ⟨n_{i↑}⟩ n_{i↓} + n_{i↑} ⟨n_{i↓}⟩ − ⟨n_{i↑}⟩⟨n_{i↓}⟩ )

i.e. each spin sees a static field set by the opposite spin's mean occupation:

  H_MF = −t Σ_{⟨ij⟩,σ}(c†_{iσ}c_{jσ}+h.c.) + U Σ_i ( ⟨n_{i↓}⟩ n_{i↑} + ⟨n_{i↑}⟩ n_{i↓} ) − U Σ_i ⟨n_{i↑}⟩⟨n_{i↓}⟩ − μ Σ_{iσ} n_{iσ}

Equivalently in (charge, spin) form, U⟨n_{iσ̄}⟩ = (U/2)(nᵢ ∓ mᵢ): the spin-σ
on-site potential is (U/2)nᵢ − (σ U/2) mᵢ with σ=±1. The −(σU/2)mᵢ piece is a
**staggered/uniform Zeeman field** generated by the magnetization — this is the
seed of magnetic order.

If a **spin-spiral / non-collinear SDW** is wanted, also keep ⟨c†_{i↑}c_{i↓}⟩
(transverse magnetization); this activates the Fock channel and the field becomes
a full 2×2 matrix in spin per site (effective field along an arbitrary axis).

### 2.3 Collinear antiferromagnet (SDW) in k-space (half filling)

On a bipartite lattice (square, with nesting wavevector Q=(π,π)) take the
staggered ansatz mᵢ = (−1)^i m, nᵢ = 1 (half filling, μ = U/2). The mean-field
Hamiltonian couples k and k+Q. Diagonalizing gives two SDW bands

  E±(k) = ±√( ε(k)² + Δ² ),   Δ = U m / 2,   ε(k) = −2t(cos kₓ + cos k_y)

with a full gap 2Δ separating lower (filled) and upper (empty) bands at half
filling. Δ ≡ Um/2 is the **antiferromagnetic gap / order parameter** (Arovas–Berg;
arXiv:2403.14768). The ferromagnetic competitor uses a uniform m (Q=0) and a rigid
spin splitting U m — favored away from half filling / off nesting (Stoner picture,
§4).

## 3. Self-consistent field (SCF) loop

The order parameters appear on both sides: they define H_MF, and they are computed
from H_MF's ground state. Solve by iteration to a fixed point.

```
# Inputs: lattice + hopping t, interaction U, filling target n_target (or μ),
#         temperature T (β=1/T), k-mesh or real-space size, mixing α∈(0,1], tol.

initialize order params  O = {n_i, m_i}        # symmetry-broken guess (see §6)
repeat:
    build H_MF(O)                              # quadratic Hamiltonian, §2
    diagonalize H_MF                           # k-space: per-k block; UHF: full real-space matrix
        -> eigenvalues E_a, eigenvectors |a⟩
    set chemical potential μ:                  # fix filling
        find μ s.t.  Σ_a f(E_a − μ) = N_target,  f(x)=1/(1+e^{βx})   (T=0: fill lowest N)
    recompute order params from occupied/weighted states:
        ⟨n_{iσ}⟩ = Σ_a f(E_a−μ) |⟨iσ|a⟩|²
        n_i = ⟨n_{i↑}⟩+⟨n_{i↓}⟩ ;  m_i = ⟨n_{i↑}⟩−⟨n_{i↓}⟩
        (spiral: also ⟨c†_{i↑}c_{i↓}⟩)
        -> O_new
    Δ = max_i |O_new_i − O_i|                  # convergence measure
    O = (1−α) O + α O_new                       # linear mixing (damping)
    if Δ < tol: break
compute E_MF (mean-field total energy incl. the −U⟨n↑⟩⟨n↓⟩ double-counting term)
```

Notes that matter for reproduction:
- **k-space vs real-space.** If the order pattern is known and periodic
  (uniform FM, Néel AFM with a 2-site cell), fold into a reduced Brillouin zone and
  diagonalize a small block per k — cheap, large effective system. For UHF
  (disorder, domain walls, inhomogeneous/unknown patterns) diagonalize the full
  N×N (per spin) real-space Hamiltonian; this lets the pattern emerge but costs
  O(N³) per iteration.
- **Chemical potential.** At fixed filling, μ is solved each iteration (bisection
  on the particle-number equation). At T=0 with a gap, μ sits in the gap.
- **Mixing.** Naive α=1 (full replacement) often oscillates or limit-cycles.
  Use linear mixing α≈0.1–0.5, or Anderson/Pulay (DIIS) acceleration for stiff
  cases.
- **Energy bookkeeping.** The mean-field energy must subtract the
  double-counted constant +U Σ_i⟨n↑⟩⟨n↓⟩ (it was added once inside each band energy
  via the field). Compare E_MF across solutions to pick the stable phase (§4).

## 4. Order parameters, broken symmetries, free-energy selection

| Phase | Order parameter | Ansatz | Where favored |
|---|---|---|---|
| Paramagnet (PM) | m=0 | uniform, spin-degenerate | small U, high T |
| Ferromagnet (FM) | uniform m≠0 | Q=0 spin splitting U m | large U + large DOS at E_F (Stoner) |
| Antiferromagnet / SDW | staggered m≠0 | Q=(π,π); Δ=Um/2 | half filling, nesting |
| Charge order / CDW | staggered n≠1 | Q≠0 density modulation | non-local V, special fillings |
| BCS pairing | Δ_pair=⟨c_{↓}c_{↑}⟩ | anomalous (Bogoliubov) channel | attractive U<0 / phonons |

**Stoner criterion (FM onset).** Linearizing the uniform-m self-consistency gives
the instability condition `U·N(E_F) > 1`, where N(E_F) is the density of states per
spin at the Fermi level: ferromagnetism appears when the interaction times the DOS
exceeds 1. The analogous SDW instability at nesting wavevector Q uses the
Q-susceptibility χ₀(Q), which diverges logarithmically for a perfectly nested
(bipartite, half-filled) Fermi surface — so the half-filled square-lattice Hubbard
model is an antiferromagnetic insulator **for arbitrarily small U>0**.

**Gap equation form.** Self-consistency for the AFM gap reduces to (T=0)

  1 = (U/N) Σ_k 1 / (2 √(ε(k)² + Δ²) )    ⇔    Δ = Δ·(U/N) Σ_k 1/(2E_k)

(finite T: insert tanh(E_k/2T) in the numerator). With a divergent nested DOS this
has a nonzero solution for any U>0; in weak coupling Δ ∝ exp(−2π√(t/U)) (Hirsch's
conjecture, proved arXiv:2501.18141) — the BCS-like essential singularity.

**Phase selection.** Multiple SCF fixed points coexist (PM, FM, AFM, …). The
physical mean-field phase is the one of **lowest free energy** F = E − T S (at T=0,
lowest E_MF). Always compute and compare F/E across the converged candidates;
the lowest one is the prediction, the others are metastable.

## 5. Weiss mean-field for spin models

### 5.1 Decoupling Sᵢ·Sⱼ

For a Heisenberg-type bond, write Sᵢ = ⟨Sᵢ⟩ + δSᵢ and drop the
fluctuation–fluctuation product δSᵢ·δSⱼ:

  Sᵢ·Sⱼ ≈ ⟨Sᵢ⟩·Sⱼ + Sᵢ·⟨Sⱼ⟩ − ⟨Sᵢ⟩·⟨Sⱼ⟩

Each spin then couples to the **molecular (Weiss) field** from its z neighbors:

  H_i^MF = h_eff · Sᵢ + const,   h_eff = J Σ_{j∈nn(i)} ⟨Sⱼ⟩  (= z J ⟨S⟩ for a uniform magnet)

with z the coordination number. The lattice problem factorizes into independent
single-site problems in a self-consistent field — the **static approximation**.

### 5.2 Self-consistent magnetization

For classical/Ising spins sᵢ=±1 with H = −J Σ_{⟨ij⟩} sᵢsⱼ − h Σ sᵢ, the single
site in field h_eff = h + zJm gives the **Curie–Weiss self-consistency**

  m = tanh( β h_eff ) = tanh( β (h + z J m) )

(Likharev/LibreTexts write h_eff = h + 2Jd·η with d the coordination number, so
"2Jd" there equals "zJ" once the bond convention is matched.) Expanding at h=0:
nonzero m appears below

  k_B T_c = z J        (Ising, mean-field; spin-½ Pauli sₓ=±1 convention)

For general spin S the tanh is replaced by the **Brillouin function** B_S:
m = S·B_S(β z J S m / …), with mean-field k_B T_c = (z J/3)·S(S+1) for Heisenberg
spins — same structure, S-dependent prefactor.

**Transverse-field Ising (TFIM)**, H = −J Σ_{⟨ij⟩} σᵢ^z σⱼ^z − Γ Σ σᵢ^x. The
single-site mean-field Hamiltonian is a spin in an effective field with longitudinal
component zJm and transverse component Γ; the moment aligns with the total field of
magnitude √((zJm)² + Γ²), giving

  m = ( z J m / √((zJm)² + Γ²) ) · tanh( β √((zJm)² + Γ²) / 2 )    (spin-½)

Two limits:
- **Quantum critical point (T=0):** expand for small m → Γ_c = z J (mean-field).
  For Γ < z J the ground state is ordered; for Γ > z J it is the polarized
  paramagnet. (Convention note: with σ Pauli operators and counting each bond once,
  some texts get Γ_c = z J/2 — track the factor from σ vs S=½ and the bond sum.)
- **Classical limit (Γ=0):** recovers k_B T_c = z J above.

### 5.3 Mean-field T_c overestimate (the headline caveat)

Mean-field assumes every neighbor sits at the *average* moment; it ignores the
correlated fluctuations that destabilize order, so it always **overestimates T_c**
(and predicts order where none exists). Benchmarks:

| System | Mean-field T_c (k_B T_c/J) | Exact | Error |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1D Ising chain (z=2) | 2 | 0 (no finite-T order) | ∞ — qualitatively wrong |
| 2D square Ising (z=4) | 4 | 2.269 (Onsager) | +76% |
| 3D simple-cubic Ising (z=6) | 6 | ≈ 4.51 | +33% |

The error shrinks as dimensionality / coordination grows (mean-field becomes exact
as z→∞ or d→∞), and is catastrophic in low d. This is the single most important
number to quote alongside any mean-field transition temperature.

## 6. Key parameters & convergence

| Knob | Role | Reproduction guidance |
|---|---|---|
| k-mesh / system size N | resolves Fermi surface, DOS, gap integral | converge Δ and E/N vs mesh; nested gap eqs need fine meshes near the FS. For UHF, finite-size + boundary (PBC vs OBC) affects the pattern. |
| Mixing α | SCF stability | start α≈0.3; lower if oscillating; use Anderson/DIIS for stiff cases. |
| Temperature T (β) | thermal occupations / T_c | T=0: fill lowest states; T>0: Fermi/tanh factors; scan T to locate T_c (where m→0). |
| Filling n / μ | which phase wins | half filling → AFM; doping → FM/spiral/PM. Solve μ each iteration to hold n. |
| Convergence tol | fixed-point criterion | tol on max order-parameter change ~1e-6–1e-8; also check energy stability. |
| Initial guess | which fixed point | **scan multiple symmetry-broken seeds** (FM, Néel, stripe, PM); SCF only finds a local fixed point. Compare free energies (§4). |

**Multiple solutions are the norm, not an error.** A converged SCF run proves only
a self-consistent fixed point exists, not that it is the lowest. Always run several
seeds and pick by free energy.

## 7. Validation / benchmarks

In rough priority order (mirrors the harness verification ladder):

1. **Non-interacting limit (U→0):** H_MF must reduce to the free tight-binding
   band; all order parameters → 0; E/N matches the exact free-fermion sum over
   filled k. First sanity check on signs and the double-counting term.
2. **Symmetry / sector:** filling held at target; total Sz (or staggered Sz) consistent with the
   ansatz; for AFM, staggered pattern on the correct sublattices.
3. **SCF convergence:** order parameters and E/N flat over the last iterations;
   re-run with finer k-mesh / tighter tol and confirm Δ, E/N are stable.
4. **Half-filled Hubbard AFM:** on the square lattice, the converged state is an
   insulating antiferromagnet with gap 2Δ for any U>0; Δ grows ∝ U at large U
   (Δ→U/2, the atomic Mott limit) and is exponentially small at weak coupling
   (Δ ∝ e^{−2π√(t/U)}). Reproduces Arovas–Berg / arXiv:2403.14768 / arXiv:2501.18141.
5. **ED upper-bound check (small systems):** the mean-field (single-Slater-determinant)
   energy is variational, so E_MF ≥ E_exact. On a small cluster, compare E_MF to
   exact diagonalization (`/method-ed`); E_MF below E_exact signals a bug.
6. **T_c overestimate, stated explicitly:** any reported T_c must carry its known
   exact comparison (§5.3 table). For 1D, report that mean-field's finite-T order
   is **spurious** (Mermin–Wagner) rather than a result.

## 8. Reproduction-sufficiency assessment

The harness knowledge base had **no mean-field material whatsoever** — there is no
`.knowledge/literature/mean-field*` folder, and the `method-mf` skill's `## Details`
section gives only scope, pitfalls, and a verification checklist, not the algorithm.
**All methodology in this file was built from web sources** (canonical lecture
notes, a review, and primary papers), listed in §9.

What the web sources fully support (reproduction-grade): the HF decoupling of
`U n↑ n↓` into Hartree/Fock channels; the collinear-AFM SDW mean-field Hamiltonian
with E±=±√(ε²+Δ²), Δ=Um/2; the AFM gap equation and its weak-coupling exponential;
the Stoner criterion; the Weiss decoupling Sᵢ·Sⱼ → molecular field; the
Curie–Weiss self-consistency m=tanh(β(h+zJm)) with k_BT_c=zJ; the TFIM mean-field
self-consistency and Γ_c; and the mean-field T_c-overestimate benchmarks (Onsager
2.269 vs 4; 3D ≈4.51 vs 6; 76% square-lattice error).

Convention caveats the reader must resolve per problem (sources differ by
factors): the factor-of-2 in h_eff (LibreTexts "2Jd" vs "zJ"), the σ-Pauli vs
S=½ normalization that shifts Γ_c between zJ and zJ/2, and the bond-counting in
T_c. These are flagged inline (§2.2, §5.2) rather than hidden. No harness-internal
anchor exists yet; a tagged SCF run (square-lattice Hubbard AFM, ED-cross-checked)
should be added to seed a *Harness anchor* once computed.
